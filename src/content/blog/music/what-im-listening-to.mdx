# What I'm Listening To Right Now

Music has always been a constant background layer to how I work and think. Here's what's been on heavy rotation lately.

## Albums on Repeat

**Burial — *Untrue* (2007)**
Still one of the greatest records ever made. The way it captures a specific kind of urban loneliness is unmatched. I come back to this every few months.

**Aphex Twin — *Selected Ambient Works Volume II* (1994)**
Background music for deep work sessions. No beats, just texture. Perfect for flow states.

**Alice Coltrane — *Journey in Satchidananda* (1971)**
For when I need something that feels genuinely spiritual. The harp arrangements are otherworldly.

**Four Tet — *There Is Love in You* (2010)**
A record that never gets old. Warm electronic music that manages to feel both intimate and expansive.

## Currently Exploring

I've been going deep on ECM Records — the German jazz label founded in 1969. The recording quality is remarkable, and the catalog is enormous. Starting points: Jan Garbarek, Keith Jarrett, Arvo Pärt.

Also getting into more industrial and post-industrial stuff. Throbbing Gristle, early Einstürzende Neubauten. There's something deeply satisfying about music that sounds like it was recorded in a factory.
